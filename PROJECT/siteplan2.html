Our Vision

Driving through Japan’s mountain passes—the legendary touge—in true MF Ghost style has always been the ultimate dream. There is a visceral pull to the idea of navigating the Hakone Turnpike or the Irohazaka slopes, hearing the high-revving echo of an engine against the rock walls. It’s about the mechanical purity and the "New Rich" versus "Old School" spirit found in Shuichi Shigeno’s universe. To perfectly clip an apex while the cherry blossoms blur past isn’t just about speed; it’s about that synchronized dance between man and machine in the birthplace of drifting culture..

How many are you taking?

Whether you are chasing the "Ghost" solo or forming your own racing team, the touge is remarkably accessible. For the purist, driving alone offers a meditative connection between the steering wheel and the winding asphalt, allowing you to master the weight transfer of your machine without distraction. However, convoying with friends transforms the mountain into a social arena, where the radio chatter and the sight of trailing headlights in your rearview mirror create an unforgettable atmosphere. Logistically, you have two paths to the summit. Bringing your own car—perhaps a tuned GT86 or a classic AE86—ensures you are driving a machine you trust implicitly. Alternatively, Japan’s premium rental services allow you to rent JDM legends specifically for these roads. Whether it’s your personal daily driver or a rented Skyline, the mountains welcome anyone with the skill to navigate them.
